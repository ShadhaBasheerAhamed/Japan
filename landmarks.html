Landmarks in Japan

Mount Fuji

Mount Fuji, Japan's tallest peak, is an iconic symbol of the country. Located on Honshu Island, this active volcano is a popular destination for climbers and tourists, offering breathtaking views and cultural significance.

Tokyo Tower

Tokyo Tower, a communications and observation tower in the heart of Tokyo, offers panoramic views of the city. Inspired by the Eiffel Tower, it is a major tourist attraction and a symbol of Japan's post-war rebirth.

Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion)

Kinkaku-ji, also known as the Golden Pavilion, is a Zen Buddhist temple in Kyoto. Covered in gold leaf, it reflects beautifully in the surrounding pond and gardens, making it one of Japan's most photographed sites.

Fushimi Inari Taisha

Fushimi Inari Taisha, located in Kyoto, is famous for its thousands of vermilion torii gates that create a mesmerizing path through the forest. This Shinto shrine is dedicated to Inari, the deity of rice and agriculture.
